## 评论
1. Musique pour la solitude d'Alice

  
Qu'est-ce que marionnettiste solitaire.   

Alice is one of those characters I relate to an actual country. In this case, France. Although I know that Alice is more based on an urban legend from Romania, her design and passionate love for puppets seem to be more french to me.   

This arrangements tends to be very repetitive, but thats how solitude works. It´s a neverending repetition.   

  

2. Resting Place

  
Her legacy won´t be forgotten. That´s for sure.   

An almost cryptic track from an almost cryptic game. I´ve only heard two arrangements of this song so far. Not many, but more than other themes have.   

  

3. A Night Flight during Day

  
She never stops wandering around.   

I don´t like Last Remote that much, but it gives a dramatic and hysterical feeling, that dissolve into a quite adorable melody.   

  

4. Dream of Living

  
No clever word for this one, sorry.   

  

5. A Phantom's Boisterous Dance

  
She´s dancing quite wild.   

Folk really suits the old PC-98 tracks, it enhances their already existant oriental feeling and touch quite well.   

  

6. Sherriff Showdown

  
She´s the sheriff, she got the star.   

I´m not quite familiar with this genre, if you even can call it that way. As usual I was experimenting around and this is the result. Enjoy!   

  

7. Endless Ocean

  
Everytime you mend pain, you will find greater treachery not far away.   

This picture of Murasa. I just had to use. And to be honest, it somehow inspired me to do this arrangement. Her eyes are so flourescend, making it the symbol of her last hope, Byakuren. So touching...   

In addition, I must say that every acoustic arrangement fits perfectly with Murasa, almost as if her character has been designed to be so.   

  

8. Temple Mandola

  
Sinless soul.   

  

9. The essence of greed

  
Greed is unstoppable.   

  

10. a story i want to tell

  
Remember...   

  

11. Hanshan's Poetry ~ Jittoku

  
Images of past´s thought.   

The title is a reference to a Taoist poet and his side-kick, Shih-Te (Jittoku in japanese), who became Immortals after being chosen as favourites of Zen painters. They both remind me of Seiga and Yoshika, in a way, and being painted has something of passing through a wall, right? Imagine Seiga floating through a canvas. leaving behind her trail manifested as a portrait of herself.   

Trippy.   

  

12. Splashing Water

  
Water coming from above.   

I´m not sure what this genre is. Is it instrumental, Jazz, Swing, or just Unplugged? I don´t know. D:
